About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Atmospheric Sciences at the University of Houston, specializing in urban micro-climate simulations, computational fluid dynamics, and predictive meteorology using deep learning approaches.

My research bridges the gap between traditional meteorological models and cutting-edge machine learning techniques to improve environmental predictions and urban planning. I have extensive experience working with large-scale numerical simulations, air quality forecasting, and developing predictive models for complex atmospheric dynamics.

With a background in mechanical engineering and a strong focus on computational methods, I bring a unique perspective to atmospheric science challenges. I am passionate about developing solutions that can help cities adapt to climate change and improve air quality for urban populations.
