About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Atmospheric Sciences at the University of Houston. My research focuses on urban micro-climate simulations, CFD, and predictive meteorology using deep learning. I also work with large-scale numerical simulations, air quality forecasting, and developing predictive models for atmospheric dynamics.

Projects

Urban Micro-Climate Simulation & Predictive Meteorology

Developed numerical solvers for PDEs and integrated Deep-CNN to forecast meteorological parameters such as cloud fraction and solar energy. Conducted large-scale simulations using OpenFOAM and WRF.

PM2.5 Forecasting with Deep Learning

Implemented hybrid models to forecast air pollution concentrations using time-series analysis and deep learning algorithms (DeepAR).

CFD Simulation of Metro Tunnel Ventilation

Optimized ventilation systems for metro tunnels using Ansys Fluent and CFD simulations to improve airflow and safety.
